The Job Board is Dead. Long Live Social Media

Are you one of the 120 million registered users of LinkedIn? Or one of the 800 million people active on Facebook? Or one of around 100 million people who regularly tweet? These numbers are staggering right?! Now think of it from the perspective of job hunting - how many people can you reach or how many people can reach you? The possibilities are pretty endless and it's relatively easily to manage if you do it right.

Don't have a LinkedIn Profile? Not active on Facebook or Twitter? You should be, especially if you are looking for new employment. More and more employers and recruiters are using sites such as LinkedIn to find the talent needed to make businesses better.

First off, can you be found? Try Google-ing yourself now. What came up? Anything? I just did it, my LinkedIn profile was second on the list, a good sign if people are looking to find out more information on me or my company. Contented Addict

"My name is Chris Sargent and I have been totally addicted to content for more than 5 years."

In very recent times there has been an explosion in the number and variety of devices that are connecting to everyone's networks. This explosion, of course, brings with it plenty of challenges and issues but far more importantly it brings massive opportunities for network providers, enterprises and end-users alike.

So, how do I quantify this 'explosion'? Well, I read recently that there will be more than 6 billion total mobile connections by the end of 2011 with more than half of them being in the APAC region! In my opinion this growth and huge number is down to the fact that most people have an insatiable addiction and desire for content. Content in all forms – Facebook, YouTube, Hulu, Twitter, iTunes, Netflix, iPlayer, you name it, people want it more…. and more and more. And they want it faster, in better quality, on more devices and they want it wherever they are in the world.Read More
